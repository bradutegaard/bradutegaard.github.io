Digital Business Cards

A mobile app that allows a business professionals to exchange contacts quickly without the need of archaic physical cards.

Find Out More

Here's How It Started

Our IT team was looking to order business cards for the new employees that we hired and I realized the limitations that I have had when exchanging business cards with other people:

  • Physical cards can easily get lost
  • There is no way of backing up critical contact info
  • Sharing business cards isn't really a thing
  • You have to manually look up social info
  • And you have to manually type out email address to send a follow up (you can always finger fumble something)

Design Process

Sketches

I usually start the design process with low fidelity wireframes. This is the way I iterate through many design options quickly.

  • This is a sketch of the home screen of the app
    • It allowed me to brainstorm quickly on how the layout of the app will look and how some of the features will work
  • This sketch was designed to focus on speed:
    • To quickly add new contacts and to quickly look up existing contacts
    • Users who need to add a contact are typically in a networking environment with a limited amount of time to exchange information so speed was the critical factor in the design
  • At the top right is the user's profile picture, where the user can click to get to their business card and edit the info they want to share with others
  • At the bottom left is a plus icon where users click to add a new business card to their collection. You can also click this to invite users to use the app.
  • Along the right-hand side is a alphabetical scroll wheel so users can search their contacts by last-name quickly

Wireframes

At the beginning of the process I created wireframes to get a feel for the layout. This way I could experiment with the UI design a little before designing a high fidelity prototype.

As a low fidelity prototype I was able to test the mutiple different designs with the user to see which one was the superior design. All 3 of the wireframes were built with Moqups, but this layout was the easiest for users to manuever with the fewest breakdowns.

High Fidelity Prototype

Once I tested out the different low fidelity prototypes on users and found the best design. I developed a high fidelity prototype on Adobe XD to present a more accurate picture of what the product would look like.
